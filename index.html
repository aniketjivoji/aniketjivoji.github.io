Engineering Philosophy

I enjoy solving engineering problems by following a structured product development approach rather than jumping directly into CAD. Every project begins with understanding the problem, defining engineering requirements, exploring multiple design concepts, evaluating trade-offs, and validating decisions through analysis, prototyping, and testing.

My interest lies in developing complete products, not just individual components. Whether designing a medical device, a robotic system, or an unmanned aircraft, I focus on creating solutions that are functional, manufacturable, reliable, and practical for real-world use.

I believe the best engineering is achieved through continuous iteration. Every prototype, failed experiment, and design revision is an opportunity to improve the final product, and I approach each project with that mindset.
